Regulatory Text

45 CFR §164.308(a)(1)(ii)(A) — Risk Analysis

A covered entity must conduct an accurate and thorough assessment of the potential risks and vulnerabilities to the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of electronic protected health information it holds.

45 CFR §164.312(a)(2)(iv) — Encryption and Decryption

A covered entity must implement a mechanism to encrypt and decrypt electronic protected health information where reasonable and appropriate, consistent with the entity’s risk analysis.

45 CFR §164.410 / §164.404 — Breach Notification

Following discovery of a breach of unsecured protected health information, a covered entity must notify each affected individual, and HHS, without unreasonable delay and in no case later than 60 days after discovery.

Compliance Scenario

Scenario

Lakeside Behavioral Health migrates its EHR platform from an on-premises server to a commercial cloud provider over a single weekend to meet a vendor-imposed end-of-support deadline. The migration team, under time pressure, does not commission a risk analysis of the new cloud environment beforehand. During the 96-hour cutover window, patient records — including psychotherapy notes — are staged in a cloud storage bucket that is later found to have been configured without encryption at rest. The bucket is corrected once discovered, three weeks after go-live, during a routine vendor security review.

Compliance Question

Was Lakeside compliant with §164.308(a)(1)(ii)(A) and §164.312(a)(2)(iv) during the migration window — and does the encryption gap trigger a Breach Notification obligation under §164.410?

Answer Choices

  • 1 Yes — the prior on-premises risk analysis still covers the data once migrated.
  • 2 No — skipping a risk analysis for the new environment is itself a gap at §164.308(a)(1)(ii)(A), and the resulting unencrypted storage window is a presumptive breach under §164.410.
  • 3 Need more information about whether any unauthorized party actually accessed the bucket.

Modular Norm Model

  1. Hover over any triangle (duty) or box (situation) to read its underlying legal text.
  2. Click a white situation box to set it TRUE (green) based on the scenario; click again for FALSE (red); click a third time to clear it.
  3. The amber box in the §164.312(a)(2)(iv) cluster is a cross‑reference — it inherits its value from whether the §164.308(a)(1)(ii)(A) risk analysis duty resolves as violated. The Breach Notification duty in turn cross‑references the encryption duty.
  4. Press Solve to propagate satisfiability up through each super‑situation. Duties recolor green (Compliant) or red (Violated); the surrounding cluster recolors to match.
  5. The Gap Register below updates automatically with every violated duty found.
