Workforce Access De-provisioning Failure

An interactive Nomos modular norm model for a HIPAA PHI gap‑analysis case study, built in the visual and reasoning style of the original Modular Norms toolchain.

Model template: Mandal, Gandhi & Siy, robinagandhi/modularnorms · Applied here to 45 CFR §164.308(a)(3)(ii)(C), §164.312(a)(1), §164.410

Regulatory Text

45 CFR §164.308(a)(3)(ii)(C) — Termination Procedures

A covered entity must implement procedures for terminating access to electronic protected health information when the employment of, or other arrangement with, a workforce member ends.

45 CFR §164.312(a)(1) — Access Control

A covered entity must implement technical policies and procedures for electronic information systems that maintain ePHI to allow access only to those persons or software programs that have been granted access rights.

45 CFR §164.410 / §164.404 — Breach Notification

Following discovery of a breach of unsecured protected health information, a covered entity must notify each affected individual, and HHS, without unreasonable delay and in no case later than 60 days after discovery.

Compliance Scenario

Scenario

A medical records clerk at Fairview Orthopedic Group is terminated for cause on a Friday afternoon. HR notifies IT the same day, but the ticket sits unprocessed over a holiday weekend and a subsequent staffing gap; the clerk’s EHR credentials are not disabled until 45 days later. An access-log audit conducted after the fact shows the clerk’s account was used to view 340 patient charts during the 45-day window, work unrelated to any active employment duties.

Compliance Question

Is Fairview compliant with the termination-procedures duty at §164.308(a)(3)(ii)(C) with respect to this clerk — and does the resulting post-termination chart access trigger obligations under §164.312(a)(1) and the Breach Notification Rule?

Answer Choices

  • 1 Yes — HR notified IT on the day of termination, which satisfies the duty regardless of processing delay.
  • 2 No — failing to revoke access at or before termination is a gap at §164.308(a)(3)(ii)(C); the subsequent unauthorized chart access is itself an access-control failure under §164.312(a)(1) that triggers Breach Notification.
  • 3 Need more information about whether the clerk viewed the charts for a legitimate purpose.

  3. The amber box in the §164.312(a)(1) cluster is a cross‑reference — it inherits its value from whether the §164.308(a)(3)(ii)(C) termination duty resolves as violated. The Breach Notification duty in turn cross‑references the access‑control duty.
  4. Press Solve to propagate satisfiability up through each super‑situation. Duties recolor green (Compliant) or red (Violated); the surrounding cluster recolors to match.
